In relation with the blacksmith's repairs.

Graphs of repairs costs = f(impact, durability)

Those graphs emphasise that the cost of repairing a pick is trilinear. It varies according to the impact level, the durability level and the product of those 2 levels. Unlike urban legends, it is not related to the sum of the upgrade levels. A pickaxe[100/0] has different repair cost than a pickaxe[50/50]. Currently, a pickaxe[100/0] has the same repair costs as a pickaxe[0/140] because those 2 upgrades are weighted equally.
The formula is : repair = round(t4/5 + t3/10 * i + t3/10 * d + d * i * t2/20) with t = tier of the pickaxe, i = the impact level and d = the durability level.
The red background is a visual clue showing how high the repair cost is compared to the repair cost of a brand new pickaxe.
